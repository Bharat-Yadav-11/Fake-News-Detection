How it works

One flow from claim to source review

FakeShield is still a review tool, not a final truth engine. The goal is to help you move from suspicion to evidence faster and with less noise.

1

Paste a clean claim

Enter one factual statement, quote, or short summary instead of multiple unrelated points.

2

Read the two verdicts

The saved model scores the wording pattern, while live lookup checks what current sources seem to support or contradict.

3

Inspect source evidence

Open the linked evidence cards, compare numbers, and confirm the publication source before trusting the claim.

Important: FakeShield helps with rapid verification triage. It should still be backed by manual review for medical, legal, financial, or other high-stakes claims.
About FakeShield

Built from your Flask fake-news project, refined into a sharper verification interface.

The original repository focused on a stored model. FakeShield extends it with a better homepage, a more usable checking workspace, live evidence cards, and an interface that feels closer to a real product.

Where it works best

Breaking public claims, company announcements, pricing headlines, and statements that should already have trustworthy reporting around them.

Where caution is required

New rumors, thinly covered local stories, and claims that require deep investigative work or primary documents beyond search results.
